Week 7 Question: Francis Kurian

One of the comments to the article "The Meaning of Decentralization" suggests that true decentralization isn’t just technical. It requires broad human inclusion (beyond a narrow builder and validator base) and solving the harder problem of real collaboration at scale. The Coasean argument is that the structure of the economy depends on the cost of coordinating people. The article “Coasean Bargaining at Scale“ suggests that if AI can drive transaction costs toward zero, society can shift from top-down regulation to massive, decentralized negotiation. But in the short term, the government should ensure the wide participation of people by providing them basic agent support to negotiate. In other words this only works if the underlying system fairly represents diverse human preferences, not just a narrow builder class.

A big question here is who will design and govern the agents that  people use? How do we ensure they truly reflect human preferences  ?
