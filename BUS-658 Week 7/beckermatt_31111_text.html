Week 7 Question: Matt Becker

"If running personal AI 'advocates' is too expensive for individuals to do themselves, will we just be trading government rules for Big Tech rules?"
The question is interesting because personal AI advocates promise to replace blunt centralized rule-making with individualized, bottom-up coordination — but if only a few firms can afford to build and operate those advocates, then power may not decentralize at all. It may simply shift from governments to platforms, turning public rules into private rules. That makes the question one of the most important governance questions in the AI era: not whether AI can represent us, but who controls the systems that do the representing.
