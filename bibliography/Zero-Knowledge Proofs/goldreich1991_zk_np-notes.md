# Proofs that yield nothing but their validity or all languages in NP have zero-knowledge proof systems

**Authors:** Oded Goldreich, Silvio Micali, Avi Wigderson  
**Year:** 1991  
**Citation key:** `goldreich1991_zk_np`  
**PDF:** local `goldreich1991_zk_np.pdf`  
**DOI:** [10.1145/116825.116852](https://doi.org/10.1145/116825.116852)  
**Google Scholar:** [Search](https://scholar.google.com/scholar?q=Proofs+that+yield+nothing+but+their+validity+zero-knowledge+NP)

## Summary

This **JACM** paper develops the theory of **zero-knowledge interactive proofs**: it proves general results on when zero-knowledge proof systems exist, including that **every language in NP** has a zero-knowledge proof assuming one-way functions or using physical randomness assumptions, and gives **unconditional** zero-knowledge protocols for **graph isomorphism** and **graph non-isomorphism** (notably the latter is not known to lie in NP). It cemented zero-knowledge as a standard tool in cryptography and complexity.
