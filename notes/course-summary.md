# BUS 658 So Far: Course Summary (Weeks 1–4)

The course is building **one coherent story**: what AI is, how big it might get, how we might align and constrain it, and how it might reshape the economy—while teaching you to **ask good questions** and use AI (including LLMs) as research tools.

---

## Week 1 — What is AI and when can we trust it?

- **Lenat & Marcus**: 16 desiderata for moving from “generative” to **trustworthy** AI.
- **Technical base**: either neural networks (3Blue1Brown) or the transformer/attention paper.
- **Context**: one of AI agents & Semantic Web, Stanford AI Index, GDPVal, or Anthropic’s Constitution.

*Theme:* Foundations of modern AI plus what “trustworthy” could mean and how we evaluate it.

---

## Week 2 — Scale and situational awareness

- **Aschenbrenner, *Situational Awareness***: how capable AI might get and how fast; strategic and geopolitical implications.
- **Mechanisms**: either scaling laws or the internals of conversational agents (e.g. ChatGPT).
- Same “context” options as Week 1.

*Theme:* AI as a **scaling phenomenon** we need to understand at a systems level.

---

## Week 3 — Safety, alignment, and epistemology

- **Anthropic Constitution**: what values and constraints we bake into models.
- **ELK (“Eliciting latent knowledge”)**: the problem of models “knowing” things they don’t say, and how to detect that.
- **One of**: Ngo (AGI safety & alignment), Singer (why Asimov’s laws are wrong), Bostrom (Doomsday Invention), or ELK prize results.

*Theme:* **How to steer and constrain AI** (constitution, alignment) and **how to detect when it’s hiding or misreporting knowledge** (ELK).

---

## Week 4 — Economics: from Malthus to the macroeconomics of AI

- **Malthus**: Principle of Population (Ch 1–2)—the trap where more food → more people → back to subsistence.
- **Acemoglu**: *The Simple Macroeconomics of AI*—how AI might affect growth, jobs, and inequality (big picture and meaning of key equations).
- **ThreeEconomicModels**: Malthusian (stagnation) → Solow-Swan (exogenous tech) → AK (ideas as the engine; growth can be endogenous).

*Theme:* **Economic frameworks** for whether AI leads to stagnation, steady growth, or a new growth regime, and who benefits.

---

## The arc so far

| Week | Theme |
|------|--------|
| 1 | **Trust & foundations** — What is AI? What would make it trustworthy? |
| 2 | **Scale & awareness** — How big and how fast might this get? |
| 3 | **Alignment & truth** — How do we align and detect hidden knowledge? |
| 4 | **Economy** — Stagnation vs growth; macro and distribution. |

In four weeks the course moves from **technical and normative foundations** → **strategic scale** → **safety and epistemology** → **economic impact**, with a steady emphasis on **asking good questions** and using LLMs and other tools to explore them. That sets you up for the rest of Unit 1 (near and far future, culture and AI) and then Unit 2 (digital platforms).
